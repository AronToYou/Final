Summary

The goal of our project is to make a simulation of fire that is as realistic as possible. We plan to study and use the generic method of physically modeling a system of interacting particles in order to acheive this, which involves solving the relevant PDEs numerically in order to predict the dynamics of the system. We also hope to explore other alternatives to a physically based rendering -- in particular we may try to exploit the dynamics exhibited by certain simplified probabilistic models in their "scaling limits", in order to render fire in a way that is not physically based but is visually appealing.

Background and Problem Description

The phenomenon of fire, like most natural processes, can be complicated to describe and intricate geometrically. Thus, if one wants to render convincing looking fire on a computer graphics display, a non-trivial approach must be adopted. In general, fluid dynamics are described by the Navier-Stokes equations. As analytically solving Navier-Stokes is intractable, numerical methods are generally used to intgrate the PDE. We believe that similar techniques will need to be used for fire simulation, although the dynamics may be more complicated. Additionally, a traditional mesh would be inefficient to modulate in time with the solutions of the NS, thus we must caste the solution into a more suitable framework.

Goals and Delivarbles

Our central goal for the project is to create a realistic simulation of fire. We propose to first implement an engine to simulate the dynamics of combustion. We will investigate primarily 2, but possibly other established techniques, to organize the framework to be rendered. We will numerically calculate the solutions to Navier-Stokes equations, which describe the motion of gaseous densities, thus giving us a description of fire to frame into a rendering engine. To measure the quality of the system we will observe the smoothness and fluidity of the simulation and compare it to real fire. Our base deliverable would be a simple video showing our simulation along with a high level overview of the math behind it, as well as the essential aspects of our implementation.

We hope to create an applet with a simple user interface that can be used to display our simulation in an interactive way. Other aspirations we have include implementing refraction from the heat on a background, and modeling the spread of fire so that our simulation is not purely stationary.
